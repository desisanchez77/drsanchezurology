Medicine has advanced. The patient experience should too.

Every patient deserves a doctor in their corner.

When patients come to me, they're often carrying more than a diagnosis. They're carrying fear, uncertainty, and questions about what their future looks like. Will I be okay? Can I trust you with this?

These are fair questions. Here's my answer.

I became a doctor because I've seen what it feels like when life is hard. Raised by a single teenage mother with three children and little resources, I learned early the importance of resilience, resourcefulness, and showing up for people when they need you most. When patients are struggling, what matters most isn't just expertise — it's knowing someone is truly in your corner.

I take pride in performing excellent surgery. But great surgery is not enough. Exceptional care means listening before recommending, explaining before operating, and making sure you understand your options — not just your diagnosis.

Medicine has advanced. The patient experience should too.

I explain. I listen. I don't rush decisions. I use the latest robotic technology when it benefits you — not because it's the newest thing, but because my patients deserve the best available. I help turn fear into understanding and uncertainty into a plan.

My goals are simple.
Cure disease when possible.
Protect your quality of life.

— Dr. Desiree Sanchez
